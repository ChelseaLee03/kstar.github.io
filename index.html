Knowledge-Guided Submap Tracking and Recovery for Monocular SLAM in Degraded Visual Conditions

K-STAR targets monocular SLAM failures on small quadruped robots in challenging conditions with motion blur, repetitive structures, and low-texture scenes. Instead of forcing uninterrupted tracking, it models visual degradation as explicit knowledge and uses state-aware recovery to preserve global consistency.

Problem Setting

Project Video

System Demo

Micro-scale quadruped robots used in pipelines and confined infrastructure often carry only a monocular camera. Fast gait dynamics and vibration degrade observations, while repetitive and texture-poor environments break conventional feature-based tracking.

Core Idea

K-STAR is failure-aware by design: it detects degraded perception, isolates local failures through submap management, and reconnects fragmented trajectories using kinematics-guided alignment across visual blind gaps.

Method Overview

Perception Layer

Infers frame reliability and degradation source from feature density and temporal persistence.

→

Decision Layer

Switches among Fine Tracking, Coarse Tracking, and Recovery through a time-aware FSM.

→

Execution Layer

Performs submap lifecycle control and greedy kinematic stitching to restore global continuity.
